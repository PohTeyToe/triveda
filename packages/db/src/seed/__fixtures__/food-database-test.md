# Canonical Food Database v1

## GRAINS

### 1. Basmati Rice

**Ayurveda:** Rasa: Sweet | Virya: Cooling | Vipaka: Sweet | Dosha: V- P- K= | Guna: light, dry
**TCM:** Nature: Neutral | Flavor: Sweet | Organs: Spleen, Stomach | Actions: tonify qi, harmonize middle
**Naturopathy:** Nutrients: B vitamins, manganese | Evidence claims: supports digestive health (moderate); low glycemic compared to other rice (strong) | Contraindications: none
**Convergence notes:** All three traditions agree on its digestive-supportive, neutral-to-cooling nature.
**Best for:** All seasons | Vata, Pitta types | Lunch

### 2. Oats

**Ayurveda:** Rasa: Sweet | Virya: Heating (cooked), Cooling (dry) | Vipaka: Sweet | Dosha: V- P= K+ | Guna: heavy, oily
**TCM:** Nature: Warm | Flavor: Sweet | Organs: Spleen, Heart | Actions: tonify qi, calm spirit
**Naturopathy:** Nutrients: beta-glucan, manganese, phosphorus | Evidence claims: lowers LDL cholesterol (strong) [PubMed:25411276]; stabilizes blood sugar (strong) | Contraindications: celiac disease (if not certified GF)
**Convergence notes:** Ayurveda and TCM both classify as warming and nourishing. Increases kapha due to heavy, oily qualities.
**Best for:** Winter, autumn | Vata types | Morning

### 3. Barley

**Ayurveda:** Rasa: Sweet, Astringent | Virya: Cooling | Vipaka: Pungent | Dosha: V= P- K- | Guna: light, dry, rough
**TCM:** Nature: Cool | Flavor: Sweet, Salty | Organs: Spleen, Stomach, Bladder | Actions: clear heat, promote urination
**Naturopathy:** Nutrients: fiber, selenium, manganese | Evidence claims: reduces cholesterol (moderate); supports gut health (moderate) | Contraindications: celiac disease
**Convergence notes:** Both traditions view barley as cooling and drying -- ideal for excess kapha or damp-heat conditions.
**Best for:** Summer, spring | Pitta, Kapha types | Any time

### 4. Buckwheat

**Ayurveda:** Rasa: Sweet, Astringent | Virya: Heating | Vipaka: Pungent | Dosha: V= P+ K- | Guna: light, dry
**TCM:** Nature: Cool | Flavor: Sweet | Organs: Spleen, Stomach, Large Intestine | Actions: clear heat, reduce dampness
**Naturopathy:** Nutrients: rutin, magnesium, fiber | Evidence claims: improves blood circulation (moderate); supports cardiovascular health (moderate) | Contraindications: buckwheat allergy
**Convergence notes:** Thermal contradiction: Ayurveda classifies as heating, TCM as cool. Both agree on drying quality.
**Best for:** Spring, summer | Kapha types | Lunch

## VEGETABLES

### 5. Spinach

**Ayurveda:** Rasa: Sweet, Astringent | Virya: Cooling | Vipaka: Pungent | Dosha: V+ P- K- | Guna: light, dry, rough
**TCM:** Nature: Cool | Flavor: Sweet | Organs: Liver, Stomach, Large Intestine | Actions: nourish blood, moisten intestines, clear heat
**Naturopathy:** Nutrients: iron, folate, vitamin K, vitamin A | Evidence claims: supports bone health (strong); anti-inflammatory (moderate) | Contraindications: kidney stones (high oxalate)
**Convergence notes:** Universal agreement on cooling, blood-nourishing properties. Ayurveda notes it can increase vata due to rough, dry qualities.
**Best for:** Summer, spring | Pitta types | Lunch, dinner

## SPICES & AROMATICS

### 6. Ginger

**Ayurveda:** Rasa: Pungent | Virya: Heating | Vipaka: Sweet | Dosha: V- P+ K- | Guna: light, dry, sharp
**TCM:** Nature: Hot | Flavor: Pungent | Organs: Lung, Spleen, Stomach | Actions: warm middle, dispel cold, resolve phlegm
**Naturopathy:** Nutrients: gingerols, shogaols, zinc | Evidence claims: anti-nausea (strong) [PubMed:10793599]; anti-inflammatory (strong) | Contraindications: bleeding disorders, gallstones
**Convergence notes:** All traditions agree on its heating, digestive-stimulating properties. One of the most universally valued spices.
**Best for:** Winter, autumn, monsoon | Vata, Kapha types | Any time

### 7. Turmeric

**Ayurveda:** Rasa: Bitter, Pungent, Astringent | Virya: Heating | Vipaka: Pungent | Dosha: V= P= K- | Guna: light, dry
**TCM:** Nature: Warm | Flavor: Bitter, Pungent | Organs: Liver, Spleen | Actions: invigorate blood, clear stasis, reduce swelling
**Naturopathy:** Nutrients: curcumin, iron, manganese | Evidence claims: anti-inflammatory (strong) [PubMed:17569207]; antioxidant (strong); supports liver health (moderate) | Contraindications: gallbladder disease, blood thinners
**Convergence notes:** All traditions value for anti-inflammatory and blood-moving properties. Ayurveda considers it tridoshic at moderate doses.
**Best for:** All seasons | All dosha types | Any time

## FRUITS

### 8. Apple

**Ayurveda:** Rasa: Sweet, Astringent | Virya: Cooling | Vipaka: Sweet | Dosha: V+ (raw) V- (cooked) P- K= | Guna: light, dry, rough
**TCM:** Nature: Cool | Flavor: Sweet, Sour | Organs: Lung, Spleen, Stomach | Actions: generate fluids, moisten lung, clear heat
**Naturopathy:** Nutrients: quercetin, pectin, vitamin C | Evidence claims: supports cardiovascular health (moderate); prebiotic fiber (moderate) | Contraindications: FODMAP sensitivity
**Convergence notes:** Ayurveda notes raw apples increase vata but cooked apples reduce it. TCM and Naturopathy both value for lung and digestive support.
**Best for:** Autumn, summer | Pitta types | Afternoon snack
